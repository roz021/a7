Lat Pulldowns

Overview

Focuses mainly on lats while using the biceps and upper back and trap muscles.

Steps

  1. Grab the bar attached to the cable and grip as if it were a ladder rung but extended.
  2. Now firmly and in a controlled movement, pull down to your chest and keep your back tight and straight.
  3. Place your butt firmly on the seat and continue pulling down while keeping your abs tight
  4. Slowly let the bar go back up while still grippin in a controlled upward motion and repeat

Pullups

Overview

A compound exercise that focuses on all of your upper back that also hits lats, traps, rear delts,and outer back.

Steps

  1. Using a pullup bar, grab it with your wrists facing away from your face.
  2. Squeeze your abs to prevent your body from moving and have a slightly wider than shoulder length grip.
  3. Squeeze your lats as well and focus on pulling your body up till your chin is slightly above the bar
  4. Hold for a moment, and while still keeping your body tight, go back down slowly.

Deadlifts

Overview

Deadlifts are a great compound exercise that mainly work your lower back while also improving bicep,hamstring and grip strength.

Steps

  1. Bend over with a bar having either no weight or weight added.
  2. Grip the bar firmly with both hands and straighten out your back.
  3. Keep your knees bent slightly and lift the bar in a vertical path.
  4. Once the bar is past your knees, swing your hips forward and straighten out your legs.
  5. Hold this for a moment and slowly bring it back down and unstraighten your legs.

Seated Cable Rows

Overview

Focuses on targeting your upper back with the delts, traps, being worked the most.

Steps
  1. Adjust the weightstack to a comfortable weight and place your buttocks on the seat.
  2. Lean over and grab the v handles and keep your back tight and straight.
  3. Lean back and while assuming a tight back position, pull the handles toward your body till it gets close to you chest
  4. Do all of this while keeping your torso stationary, hold for a moment and bring handle back and then forth.
