Abs

Crunches

Overview

Crunches are where you lie on the ground with your legs bent in an upside down v pose. Then you squeeze your abs and raise up a bit to get your upper body off the ground about 30 degrees and then go back down.

Steps

  1. Lay flat on your back with knees bent feet flat on floor, hips width apart
  2. Place hands behind your head so your thumbs are behind your ears

Russian Oblique Twists

Put your body in a sitting position with your upper body at an 45 degree angle and legs at an upside down v pose. Keep your arms close to your chest and twist in a left and right motion. You will feel a burn on the sides of your core.

Situps

Similar to crunches, sit down with your butt planted on the ground and legs in a upside down v position. this time, clench your abs and go all the way up until your chest touches your knees. Then go back down and that will be one rep.

Decline Situps

A slight variation to the situp. This time, using a decline bench, place your body and hook your legs through the leg rests and enact the situp position. This exercise focuses more on your lower abs.

Back

Lat Pulldowns

This exercise works on your lats, the sides closest to your chest and full upper body. Grab the bar attached to the cable and grip as if it were a ladder rung but extended. Now firmly and in a controlled movement, pull down to your chest and keep your back tight and straight. This will result in your muscles activating and feeling a slight burn on the sides of your upper body.

Pullups

Find a pullup bar and then grab it with your wrists facing away from your face. Make sure you have a slightly wider than shoulder length grip and squeeze your abs while concentrating your strength to lifting your body up with a tight grip on the bar. This exercise works out your upper back with your traps, abs, and rear delts being activated.

Deadlifts

Grip the bar like you would with pullups. Make sure your back is straight and when you lift off, keep your legs bent and once the bar gets past your knees, straighten your legs up. This works out your lower back, a little bit of grip strength, and hamstrings.

Seated Cable Rows

This exercise focuses on targeting your upper back with the delts, traps, being worked the most. A good exercise for eliminating swing motion and taking as much momentum out of the equation.

Bicep

Hammer Curls

Overview

It's Hammer Time! Works on the peak of your bicep to give it that nice curvature.

Steps

  1. Grab a pair of dumb bells while standing up straight. Keep your abs, elbows and arms tight.
  2. Imitate the motion you would for swining a hammer but keep it slow and in a controlled motion
  3. Hold for a moment and go back up.

Concentration Curls

Overview

A good isolation exercise that focuses solely on the bicep as you take out momentum and abs in the equation.

Steps

  1. Place your buttocks on a bench or chair.
  2. With a dumb bell in hand, place the back of your arm against your leg and keep it in a perpendicular motion.
  3. Curl the dumbbell upwards as you would for any normal bicep curl.
  4. Hold for a moment, and slowly bring it down in a controlled motion.

Preacher Curls

Overview

Good for isolating outer bicep as well as giving you the pump for your peak.

Steps

  1. Using the preacher bench, grab either a dumbbell or a weighted barbell.
  2. With your buttocks firmly placed on the seat, make sure your arms are fully on the bench.
  3. Gripping the dumbbell/weighted barbell firmly, lift in an upward motion and hold for a moment.
  4. After a moment has passed, slowly bring it down in a controlled motion.

Spider Curls

Overview

Another variation of the bicep curl. Isolates and targets your biceps to burn harder.

Steps

  1. Use an inclined bench or change the position of a bench to be inclined.
  2. Use either dumb bells or a weighted barbell and place your chest and abs firmly on the bench.
  3. In a slow and controlled motion, curl the dumb bell/weighted barbell up and hold for a moment.
  4. After a moment has passed, slowly bring it back down and repeat.

Calf

Standing Calf Raises

Overview

Standing Calf Raises are a good way to grow your calves without placing too much pressure on your body.

Steps

  1. Grab a pair of dumb bells and grip them to your sides while keeping your body stiff.
  2. Lift up your heeels while keeping your toes on the ground.
  3. Hold for a moment while heels elevated in the air.
  4. Slowly bring back down to the ground and repeat.

Calf Leg Presses

Overview

Great way to grow calves with a heavier weight or lower weight for volume sets.

Steps

  1. Find a leg press machine and sit down on it.
  2. Plant your feet halfway where your from half your feet to toes are on the platform.
  3. Release the safeguards and slowly bring your knees in and hold for a moment.
  4. Push up with your toes till you feel your calves stretch.

Seated Calf Raises

Overview

Seated Calf raises are a good way to load weight without putting a strain on your arms.

Steps

  1. Adjust the weights to a comfortable or challenging weight and place your buttocks on the seat.
  2. Keep your upper body straght and your feet halfway planted on the foot rests.
  3. Pull the safeguard to the side and lift your legs up by using your toes and go all the way up and down slowly.

Farmer Walks on toes

Overview

Farmer Walks on Toes really make your calves burn because you're constantly engaging the muscle.

Steps

  1. Grab a set of dumb bells and have your arms to the side of your body
  2. Walk around only on your toes and do a circuit.

Cardio

Sprints

Overview

Sprints are good for training explosive speed, quads, and improving heart rate.

Steps

  1. Have your arms tucked to the side of your body and in a crounched forward motion.
  2. Push off the ground with your legs and have your arms cycle up and down while running.

Ellipticals

Overview

Known as the knee saving cardio since your feet don't pound on the ground.

Steps

  1. Find an elliptical machine and plant your feet on the designated foot areas.
  2. Turn on the machine and start pedaling.

Stairmasters

Overview

A good exercise to punish your legs and get your glutes and quads quivering.

Steps

  1. Find a stairmaster machine and turn it on.
  2. Place your feet on the stairs and start walking like you're going up stairs.

Rowing

Overview

Good workout for making you keep a steady tempo while also working out your upper back.

Steps

  1. Find a rowing machine and turn it on.
  2. Set your feet on the foot guards and strap up.
  3. Grab the handles and keep your back straight and pull back.
  4. After pulling back, lean forward with the handles in hand and repeat the rowing motion.

Chest

Barbell Bench Press

Overview

A compound exercise that focuses mainly on chest with a bit of triceps. You grab the barbell while you are seated lying face up on the bench and push off and let it come down and then push back up to complete one rep.

Steps

  1. Place your body flat against the bench and lay down face up.
  2. Roll your shoulders back to push your chest up.
  3. Grab the barbell and lift it up and slowly let it come down till it touches your chest.
  4. Hold for a moment and bring back up.

Dumb bell Incline Press

Overview

This focuses on form as well as fixing instability within your chest.

Steps

  1. Grab a pair of dumb bells and find an incline bench and sit with your body flat on the bench and face up.
  2. Lift the dumb bells up and bring them down till your arms form a 90 degree angle.
  3. Hold for a moment and push back up.

Seated Cable Rows

Overview

A good way to get volume into your chest by allowing you to do multiple reps.

Steps

  1. Place a chair/bench near a cable machine.
  2. Position and anchor the cables to where you're in a comfortable position.
  3. Sit in the bench/chair and grab the handles (attached to the cables).
  4. Pull in an inward motion that makes you compress your chest.

Seated Machine Chest Press

Overview

A good way to fix muscle instability and focus on one pec at a time.

Steps

  1. Find a seated machine press.
  2. Adjust the weight stack and place your buttocks on the seat.
  3. Imitate the motion of doing a bench press and keep your abs tight and back planted firmly on the chair.
  4. Push outwards and hold for a moment, then slowly bring it back.

Forearm

Seated Wrist Flexions

Overview

Focuses on inner forearms and useful for improving grip strength.

Steps

  1. Grab a pair of dumb bells or a weighted barbell.
  2. Find a chair or bench and sit down on it.
  3. Place your arms inbetween your legs and when gripping the dumb bells/weighted barbell, have your fingers facing towards you.
  4. Curl your wrists in an upwards motion and hold for a momement.
  5. Slowly curl back down.

Seated Wrist Extensions

Overview

Focuses on wrists while also working your outer forearms.

Steps

  1. Find a bench or chair with your dumb bells/weighted barbell.
  2. Sit down and grip the dumb bells/weighted barbell and have the back of your hands facing towards you.
  3. Have your forearms rested on your knees and curl in an upward motion.
  4. Hold for a moment and slowly curl back down.

Dumbbell Farmer Walks

Overview

A great way to keep your forearms engaged to improve grip strength and give volume to your forearms.

Steps

  1. Grab a set of dumb bells and grip them to the side of your body.
  2. Keep your body straight and walk around while tightly maintaining grip.

Pullup Bar Hangs

Overview

Improves Forearm strength while also working on your lats.

Steps

  1. Find a pullup bar and grip it while engaging your abs.
  2. Hang on tightly and hold the position.

Glute

Barbell Back Squats

Overview

A compound exercise that works out abs, quads, and glutes. Place the bar on your traps and point your toes in an outward angle. Now go down like you're sitting in a chair and then stop at a 90 degree angle. Then go back up and repeat. You will feel this is in your glutes and quads.

Steps

  1. Place the bar on your traps & point your toes in an outward angle.
  2. Engage your abs & quads & mimic the motion that you're sitting down on a chair.
  3. Stop at a 90 degree angle and hold for a moment.
  4. Slowly go back up to the top while keeping your abs engaged and stop when you are standing again.

Barbell Glute Bridges

Overview

Focuses soley on glutes while giving it the volume you want.

Steps

  1. Find a bench and have a barbell with no weight or weighted.
  2. Place your upper body against the side of the bench and have the barbell resting on your hips.
  3. Assume the up position of a russian oblique twist and lift your butt off the ground.
  4. Hold for a moment and slowly bring it back down.

Clamshell

Overview

A good rehab/stretch for activating your hip flexors and glutes.

Steps

  1. Lay down on the floor in a fetal position.
  2. Now in position, open up the leg that is not on the ground in a clamshell motion.
  3. Hold for a moment up in the air and place back down slowly.

Dolphin Kick

Overview

Works out glutes and has a bit of abs mixed in. Have a box and place your stomach on it. Keep your body elevated and strike a superman pose. Maintain the pose and then lift your legs up behind you and you will feel it all in your glutes.

Steps

  1. Lay flat on the ground and assume a superman position.
  2. Keep your arms and legs elevated and maintain the pose.
  3. Lift your legs up behind you and feel the burn in the glutes.

Shoulder

Military Overhead Press

Overview

Compound exercise that works out traps, delts, and abs.

Steps

  1. Use a barbell and be standing straight with your back and abs engaged.
  2. Grip it with the back of your hands facing you.
  3. Push the barbell up in a vertical motion till it goes fully past your head and your arms are in a v shape.
  4. Hold for a moment and bring it down slowly.

Dumb Bell Lateral Raises

Overview

Targets the big head of your shoulders for giving you the broad shoulder look.

Steps

  1. Grab a pair of dumb bells and hold them laterally to the side.
  2. Lift them up laterally until it rises parallelly to the ground.
  3. Hold for a moment and bring it back down to your sides.

Seated Overhead Presses

Overview

Meant for fixing muscle imbalance and helps isolate the shoulders.

Steps

  1. Find a chair and sit down with a pair of dumb bells.
  2. Lift the dumb bells up so they are at a 90 degree angle and your body looks like a trident.
  3. Push up all the way till your arms are extended straight and hold for a moment.
  4. Bring your arms back down slowly until you are back at a 90 degree angle.

Arnold Presses

Overview

Arnold Schwarzenegger's infamous variation to the overhead press.

Steps

  1. Assume the same position as the seated overhead press.
  2. Keep your arms at a 90 degree angle, then press and bring back down.
  3. Now swing your arms inwards and outwards slowly and press again.

Thigh

Romanian Deadlifts

Overview

A killer exercise for getting volume in your hamstrings only.

Steps

  1. Grab a pair of dumb bells and keep your legs slightly bent.
  2. Have the weights in front of you and keep your bnack straight.
  3. While maintaining that pose, slowly pull the weights up like you are picking something up from the ground.
  4. Hold for a moment and then slowly come back down while still keeping your back straight and legs slightly bent.

Leg Press

Overview

Leg presses are a good way to get volume in your thighs.

Steps

  1. Using a leg press, place your body onto the chair and back on the bench.
  2. Place your feet in an outward motion and have a wider stance like you are squatting on the platform.
  3. Push the safeguards to the side and bring your legs in and hold for a moment.
  4. Slowly bring your legs back out.

Leg Extensions

Overview

Pure thigh isolation and allows for fixing muscle imbalance.

Steps

  1. Sit down on the leg extension machine and place your feet in the foot guards.
  2. Push your legs up and hold for a moment.
  3. Slowly bring them back down.

Goblet Squats

Overview

Exclusively targets your thighs and works on volume building.

Steps

  1. Hold a dumb bell/kettle bell in your hands by your chest.
  2. While maintaining your dumb bell/kettle bell in that position, slowly squat down.
  3. Hold for a moment and then slowly come back up.

Tricep

Dumbbell Kickbacks

Overview

Allows you to focus on one arm or both and is meant for fixing muscle irregularity and instability.

Steps

  1. Grab a pair of dumb bells and while standing, lean forward a bit.
  2. Have your arms at a 90 degree angle.
  3. Now pull your arms up while keeping your elbow tight until its parallel to the ground.
  4. Hold for a moment and then slowly bring it back down to 90 degrees and repeat.

Cable Pulldowns

Overview

Pulldowns hit both the small and big heads of your triceps for strength and volume building.

Steps

  1. Find a rope attachnment to a cable machine.
  2. Grab the ropes by the ends and bend forward slightly.
  3. Pull down on the rope and keep your elbows tight while engaging your lats.
  4. Hold for a moment and slowly bring back up.

Dumbbell Incline Press

Overview

This focuses on form as well as fixing instability within your triceps.

Steps

  1. Grab a pair of dumb bells and find an incline bench
  2. Almost like you're doing a bench press, twist the dumb bells up and bring them down to a 90 degree angle.
  3. When you go up and down, make sure you twist so your triceps are the ones being activated.

Bench Dips

Overview

A good finisher for triceps. Place your hand behind your back and onto the side of a bench, have your upper body straight and legs straight with the heels of your feet only touching the ground. Now slowly lower yourself with your arms and you will feel the burn when going down and back up.

Steps

  1. Place your hands behind your back on the side of the bench.
  2. Have your upper body straight and legs striaght with heels of feet only touching the ground.
  3. Now slowly lower yourself with your arms and hold for a moment.
  4. Slowly go back up.
